Rethinking cell & molecular biology curricula

This project reflects many fruitful discussions with faculty from the School of Education (CU Teach), the science education community., and the HHMI CourseSource project team, . Our goal is a conceptually coherent and pedagogially effective curricula in modern biology

We build on lessons learned in the development and of biofundamentals, an "alternative" introduction to modern biology (read a student's view of the course here). Biofundamentals itself was inspired by observations made during the National Science Foundation (NSF)-funded Biology Concept Inventory (BCI) project, a collaboration with Kathy Garvin-Doxas. 

Equally critical has been the lessons learned during the development of CLUE: Chemistry, Life, the Universe & Everything (and OrganicCLUE), an NSF-funded general chemistry curriculum. CLUE and biofundamentals depend upon graphics-based formative assessments delivered through the beSocratic, again made possible by the briliant programming efforts of Sam Bryfczynski and funds from the NSF.

Going forward, we are introding AI tutors, outcomes analysts, and consultants to provide students and teachers with meaningful feedback.

Comments on the ideas presented here are very welcome
